<p><p>The trauma fixation equipment market consists of various types designed for stabilizing fractures. Internal fixation includes plates, screws, nails, and other devices that are surgically implanted to hold fractured bones together. Plates provide surface support, screws secure fragments, and nails are inserted into the marrow cavity. External fixation involves devices attached outside the body, stabilizing fractures while allowing for soft tissue management. Each type offers distinct advantages based on the fracture's location, severity, and patient needs, ensuring effective treatment outcomes.</p></p>
